<p><p>Tissue glue and bio-adhesive sealants are used in various medical procedures to seal tissues and promote healing. Protein-based sealants include collagen-based, thrombin-based, fibrin, and gelatin-based sealants. Collagen-based sealants are derived from collagen proteins, while thrombin-based sealants work by activating the body's clotting mechanism. Fibrin sealants are made from fibrinogen and thrombin to form a clot, while gelatin-based sealants use gelatin as the adhesive agent. Other types of bio-adhesive sealants may include synthetic polymers or other naturally occurring substances.</p></p>
