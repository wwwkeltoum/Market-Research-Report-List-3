<p><p>Non-resilient floor covering refers to flooring materials that do not have the ability to bounce back to their original shape after being compressed or deformed. Examples of non-resilient floor coverings include hardwood, laminate, ceramic tile, and stone. These types of flooring are known for their durability and longevity compared to resilient floor coverings such as vinyl or linoleum.</p><p>The non-resilient floor covering market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 7.8% during the forecast period. The market growth can be attributed to factors such as increasing demand for aesthetically pleasing and durable flooring solutions in residential and commercial spaces. Consumers are increasingly opting for non-resilient floor coverings due to their ability to enhance the overall look and feel of a space while also providing long-lasting performance.</p><p>One of the latest trends in the non-resilient floor covering market is the increasing popularity of sustainable and eco-friendly flooring options.
