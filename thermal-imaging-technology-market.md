<p><p>Thermal imaging technology is divided into two main types: uncooled and cooled. Uncooled thermal imaging devices use sensors that operate at ambient temperature, making them more cost-effective and energy-efficient. They are typically used for consumer applications like smartphones and car night-vision systems. On the other hand, cooled thermal imaging devices use sensors that are cooled to very low temperatures, providing higher image quality and sensitivity. They are mainly used in military, surveillance, and research applications where optimal performance is crucial.</p></p>
