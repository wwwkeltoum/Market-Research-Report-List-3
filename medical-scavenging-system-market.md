<p><p>Medical scavenging systems are essential in healthcare settings to safely remove waste anesthetic gases and prevent exposure to healthcare workers. There are three main types of scavenging systems: active vacuum systems, which actively remove gases through suction; passive vacuum systems, which rely on the flow of gases to remove waste; and centralized systems, which collect gases from multiple sources for centralized processing. Each system has its own advantages and is chosen based on the specific needs of the healthcare facility.</p></p>
