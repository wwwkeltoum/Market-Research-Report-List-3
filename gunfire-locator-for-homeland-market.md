<p><p>Gunfire locators for homeland security come in two main market types: acoustic and optical. Acoustic gunfire locators use sensors to detect the sound of gunfire and determine the location of the shooter. These systems are effective in urban areas with high levels of background noise. On the other hand, optical gunfire locators use cameras and other visual sensors to track the flash and smoke of gunfire. These systems are useful in rural or open areas where visual detection is more reliable. Both types of gunfire locators serve as critical tools for detecting and responding to gun violence in homeland security.</p></p>
