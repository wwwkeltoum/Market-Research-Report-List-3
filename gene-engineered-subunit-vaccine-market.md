<p><p>Gene Engineered Subunit Vaccines can be categorized into two main types: Therapeutic and Preventative. Therapeutic vaccines are designed to treat existing diseases by stimulating the immune system to target specific antigens associated with the disease. Preventative vaccines, on the other hand, aim to prevent diseases by priming the immune system to recognize and fight off potential pathogens before infection occurs. Both types of vaccines utilize genetically engineered subunits of pathogens to trigger an immune response, offering innovative approaches to disease prevention and treatment.</p></p>
