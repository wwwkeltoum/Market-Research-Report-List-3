<p><ul><li>Total Replacement</li><li>Partial Replacement</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Total Replacement refers to the complete replacement of a joint with an artificial prosthesis, commonly used in severe cases of joint degeneration. Partial Replacement involves replacing only a part of the joint, preserving healthy tissue and ligaments. Total Replacement is more common for advanced arthritis and joint damage, while Partial Replacement is often used for less severe cases where only a portion of the joint is affected. Both types of joint replacement aim to improve mobility, reduce pain, and enhance overall quality of life.</p></p>
