<p><p>Intubation cannulas are medical devices used to establish an airway in patients who are unable to breathe on their own. There are three main types of intubation cannulas: Tracheostomy cannulas are inserted directly into the trachea through a surgical opening in the neck. Nasopharyngeal cannulas are inserted through the nose and into the pharynx. Oropharyngeal cannulas are inserted through the mouth and into the pharynx. Each type of cannula is used in different clinical scenarios based on the patient's condition and airway requirements.</p></p>
