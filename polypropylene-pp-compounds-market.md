<p><ul><li>Homopolymer</li><li>Copolymer</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Polypropylene (PP) compounds are commonly categorized into two main types based on their polymer structure: homopolymer and copolymer. Homopolymer PP compounds consist of only propylene monomer units, resulting in a polymer with a high melting point and stiffness ideal for applications requiring rigidity and strength. Copolymer PP compounds, on the other hand, contain additional monomer units such as ethylene or other alpha-olefins, providing improved impact resistance and flexibility compared to homopolymer PP compounds.</p></p>
